the problem

AI is breaking the old path from school to work.

Students are being asked to make life-shaping education and career decisions in a world where the rules are changing faster than schools, parents, and career systems can keep up.

  • Students do not know what careers are safe or what skills matter most now.
  • Parents do not know what their children should study or how to evaluate progress.
  • Career centers cannot personalize guidance for thousands of students.
  • Recruiters are overwhelmed with noise and lack a trusted signal for early-career talent.
why this matters
The world has a talent signaling problem. Students do not know how they are evaluated, and employers do not have an efficient way to identify high-potential talent.

dilly solves this by creating the score, coaching layer, learning layer, and matching layer that connect education to employment.

That makes dilly much more than a resume tool. It becomes infrastructure.

market

a large consumer wedge with enterprise expansion.

dilly starts with students and parents, where urgency is high and buying decisions are emotional. It then expands into universities, recruiters, and employers, creating multiple revenue streams around the same data layer.
The most important part of this market is that it is not just the United States. India is one of the largest education markets in the world, with hundreds of millions of students and extremely high demand for upward mobility, international education, and technology careers. A platform that helps students choose careers, improve profiles, and get jobs has significantly higher volume potential in India than in the US.
The long term vision is a US + India platform where US students drive revenue per user, and India drives scale, data, and distribution.
