Inside the audit

Three lenses, one story

Recruiters skim for rigor, impact, and proof. Dilly scores each separately so you know whether the problem is your GPA line, your verbs, or your projects.

◎

Smart

Coursework, rigor, and academic signals that match competitive applicants in your field. See if you are underselling depth or missing obvious credentials.

◇

Grit

Leadership, ownership, and quantified impact. Vague bullets tank this score fast. Dilly points to the lines that need numbers, scope, and outcomes.

▣

Build

Proof you can ship: projects, internships, research, clinical hours, portfolios. This is where “almost competitive” becomes “shortlisted.”

From upload to action plan

Upload once. Dilly parses structure and content, maps signals to your track, and returns dimension scores plus ranked reasons. You are not left with a PDF and a shrug.

Every weak signal ties to a concrete edit: swap a verb, add a metric, surface a project, or reorder a section so the skim hits the right beats in the first six seconds.

  • Gap summary per dimension with example rewrites
  • Comparison hooks for internship readiness later in the app
  • History as you iterate so you can see movement week over week

Flow

How it feels in the product

A guided path from blank anxiety to a prioritized list of edits.

  1. Upload your resume in PDF or DOCX. Dilly normalizes layout noise so scoring focuses on substance.
  2. Review Smart, Grit, and Build with short explanations. Tap any dimension to drill into bullets that dragged it down.
  3. Prioritize the top fixes Dilly ranks by lift versus effort. Small wins surface first so momentum builds.
  4. Re-score after edits in the resume editor or a fresh upload. Watch the needle move on the dimensions that matter for your goals.

Why a multi-axis score beats one “strength” meter

A single score tells you nothing about whether you are weak on proof versus weak on story. Students with the same headline number often need opposite strategies. Dilly separates those threads so you do not optimize the wrong thing.

When you open a real internship listing, those same dimensions power Ready, Almost, and Gap. The score you build on the resume page is the same language you will see next to roles you care about.

That continuity is the point: fewer surprises, less guesswork, and a clear line from diagnosis on the page to strategy in applications.
