One ladder: elementary → high school → college

Duolingo’s lesson isn’t “green buttons.” It’s continuity: the same brand promise and interaction grammar, with surfaces that match each age’s gatekeepers (parents, teachers, admissions).

Elementary · curiosity safe

Play that secretly teaches

Short loops, characters, and immediate wins. Parents need to trust screen time; kids need autonomy without reading dense instructions.

  • Bite-sized sessions (attention-matched)
  • Visual progress, not spreadsheets
  • Zero shame on wrong answers — retry is the game
↓
High school · identity + pace

Social energy without chaos

Teens sniff out anything that talks down to them. The UI stays friendly; the copy gets honest. Competition and streaks optional, not humiliating.

  • Clear “why this matters for my future” hooks
  • Teacher-visible progress (Schools analog)
  • Mobile-first — that’s where they live
↓
College · stakes + proof

Same habit, harder outputs

Undergrads need efficiency: show me what to do today, how I’m trending, and what I can show an employer. The product matures; the brand doesn’t need to get boring.

  • Portfolio- or credential-ready artifacts
  • Explainable feedback (especially for AI)
  • Respect for time — fewer gimmicks, same clarity
