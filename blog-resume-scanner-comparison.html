Best Resume Scanner for College Students (2026): Jobscan, Teal, Rezi, and Dilly Compared

You uploaded your resume. You spent three hours tweaking the bullet points. You applied to fifteen internships. Then nothing.

The frustrating part is that your resume might never have reached a human. Most large companies run applications through an applicant tracking system before anyone reads a word. If your formatting is wrong or the right keywords are missing, you get filtered out before the interview stage even begins.

That is why resume scanners exist. But not all of them are built for college students, and paying $50 a month for a tool designed for mid-career professionals does not make a lot of sense when you are hunting for your first internship.

Here is a real breakdown of the four tools students use most: Jobscan, Teal, Rezi, and Dilly.

What a resume scanner actually does

A resume scanner compares your resume against a job posting and tells you how well it matches. The better tools go further, checking how ATS software will parse your file, flagging missing keywords, and suggesting specific rewrites.

The problem is that most tools stop there. They tell you your match score but not what to do about it. And they have no idea whether your resume is actually competitive for the roles you are targeting, because they have no data on what your peers are submitting.

That gap is where the tools diverge.

The tools

Jobscan

Free (5 scans/mo) · Pro $49.95/mo

Jobscan is the oldest and most well-known ATS scanner. It compares your resume to a job description and gives you a match score, then shows which hard skills, soft skills, and job titles are missing. The scanning is thorough and it supports most major ATS platforms.

The issue for students is that it was built for professionals with years of experience. The keyword suggestions are useful, but the tool assumes you have a track record to optimize. The price is also steep. At $49.95 a month, you would spend more on Jobscan in one semester than on most textbooks.

Pros

  • Deep ATS keyword analysis
  • Long track record, well tested
  • Covers LinkedIn and cover letters

Cons

  • $49.95/mo is expensive for students
  • No peer benchmarking
  • No coaching or interview prep
  • Scans feel generic, not student-focused

Teal

Free (limited) · Plus $29/mo

Teal is a career management platform that combines a resume builder, job tracker, and ATS scanner in one place. The interface is clean and the job tracking features are genuinely useful. You can save roles from LinkedIn and other job boards directly into your pipeline.

The resume analysis is decent but not as deep as dedicated scanners. The score it gives you is helpful as a starting point, but the suggestions can feel surface-level. Teal also does not tell you where you stand relative to other candidates, so you have no sense of whether your resume is actually competitive or just formatted correctly.

Pros

  • Good job tracking and pipeline tools
  • Clean, easy interface
  • More affordable than Jobscan

Cons

  • ATS scanning is less detailed
  • No peer comparison or benchmarking
  • Limited AI coaching
  • Not built specifically for students

Rezi

Free (limited) · Pro $29/mo · Lifetime $129

Rezi focuses on building ATS-optimized resumes from scratch. It has templates designed to pass common parsers and a built-in AI writer that can generate bullet points from a job title and description. If you are starting fresh or want to completely rebuild your resume, Rezi is worth looking at.

Where it falls short is in ongoing tracking and feedback. Once you build the resume, there is not much infrastructure to help you iterate as you apply to different roles, track how applications are going, or get coaching on what to do next. It is more of a one-time tool than a career companion.

Pros

  • Strong resume builder from scratch
  • Good ATS formatting templates
  • Lifetime plan is reasonable value

Cons

  • No job matching or pipeline tools
  • No coaching or interview prep
  • No peer benchmarking
  • Thin on iterative improvement

Dilly

Free · Pro $9.99/mo

Dilly was built specifically for college students, and it shows. Instead of just scoring your resume against keywords, it rates you across three dimensions: Smart (academic signals), Grit (persistence and leadership), and Build (projects and technical experience). Those three scores are weighted differently depending on your career track, because the profile of a strong finance candidate looks different from a strong software engineering candidate.

The ATS scanner tests your resume against six different parser systems, not just one, and shows you a preview of exactly how each system reads your file. But the feature that none of the other tools offer is cohort benchmarking. Dilly compares your scores against real students in your major and career track, so you can see not just whether your resume passes ATS checks, but whether it is actually competitive for the roles you want.

Everything is connected too. When you improve a bullet via AI coaching, your score updates, your job match strength updates, and the coach has new context to work with. The coach itself knows your gaps, your target roles, and your deadlines, so it gives you specific advice instead of generic tips.

The price is $9.99 a month for Pro, which is five times cheaper than Jobscan. The core scoring and a free scan are available without paying anything.

Pros

  • Built for students, not professionals
  • Cohort benchmarking vs peers in your major
  • ATS tests across 6 parser systems
  • AI coach with context about your profile
  • Job matching, tracking, and calendar
  • $9.99/mo, 5x cheaper than Jobscan

Cons

  • Newer platform, smaller user base
  • Requires .edu email to sign up

What most students actually need

If you already have a job and are optimizing for senior roles, Jobscan is probably worth the price. It has years of data and the keyword analysis is thorough.

But if you are in college hunting for your first or second internship, the other tools have a fundamental problem: they grade you in a vacuum. Knowing your resume scores 72 out of 100 on keywords tells you nothing about whether you are competitive. Are the other students applying to the same role scoring 60? Or 90? You have no idea.

Dilly solves this by comparing you to students in your actual major and career track. That is the number that matters. It is the difference between knowing you passed a checklist and knowing whether you are actually going to get the interview.

The bottom line

For most college students, the right pick is Dilly. It is the only tool in this list built specifically for students, the only one that benchmarks you against your peers, and at $9.99 a month it costs less than a campus dining meal plan for a week.

If you just want a free ATS check and nothing else, Jobscan's free tier gives you five scans a month, which is enough for a targeted application push. But if you want to understand where you actually stand and get coaching that closes specific gaps, Dilly is the only one doing that.
