Built by a student
who got frustrated

Dilly exists because career centers can't scale, resume tools don't understand context, and students deserve better than generic advice.

Here's the truth about career services on campus: one advisor for hundreds of students. Office hours that fill up in minutes. And when you finally get a meeting, the advice is the same for everyone — "use action verbs" and "quantify your impact." That's not coaching. That's a template.

Recruiting is getting harder every year. The class of 2026 is graduating into the most competitive internship market in history. Companies are using AI to filter candidates before a human ever sees them. Students are applying to 50, 100, 200 positions and hearing nothing back. The system is broken and it's not their fault.

"I watched students around me — smart, hardworking people — get ghosted by companies because their resume didn't pass an ATS filter. Or because they didn't know how to frame their experience. Not because they weren't qualified. Because nobody showed them how the game works."

Dilly started as a simple question: what if you could see your resume the way a recruiter sees it? Not just keywords or formatting — but the actual signals that hiring managers look for. Academic rigor. Leadership and ownership. Real proof of building things.

That became the three-dimension score: Smart, Grit, and Build. Three numbers that tell you exactly where you stand. Not compared to some abstract ideal — compared to what real companies actually look for, weighted by your career track.

Then it grew. If you know your gaps, you need a plan to fix them. So we added AI coaching that knows your full context. Then internship matching that shows your fit per role. Then ATS scanning because it doesn't matter how good your resume is if the robots reject it. Then a resume editor with live scoring. Then a tracker, a calendar, a leaderboard.

Every feature connects to one thing: your Dilly Score. Everything you do in the app — editing a bullet, prepping for an interview, applying to a job — makes you more competitive. And you can see it in the number.

"The career center in your pocket. Open 24/7. Knows your resume, your scores, your applications, your deadlines. Gives you specific advice, not generic templates. For less than a DoorDash order per month."

That's Dilly. Not another resume scanner. Not another job board. A system that makes you better — measurably, visibly, continuously.
