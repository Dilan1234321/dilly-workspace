A fairer playing field

Real students. Not noise.

Open job boards let anyone sign up — so inboxes fill with spray-and-pray apps, bots, and profiles that do not hold up. Real students compete in a polluted pool. Dilly is built for the cohort campus hiring is supposed to serve.

“Roughly six in ten applications we see through the big open boards are junk — automated volume, filler, or materials that do not match reality. The people who did the work still vanish in that pile.”

Hiring manager, early-career recruiting

Why applying can feel hopeless

Platforms like LinkedIn and Handshake optimize for reach, not proof of student status. That openness is why spam applications and questionable profiles are common — and why silence in your inbox is not always about you. It is often a numbers game where signal drowns.

How Dilly changes the math

You need a .edu email to sign up. Everyone on Dilly is a verified student at the gate. When recruiters use Dilly or see a Dilly applicant, they are not guessing whether they are looking at the open-internet flood — they are looking at enrolled students. Your application competes with peers, not with automation at scale.
