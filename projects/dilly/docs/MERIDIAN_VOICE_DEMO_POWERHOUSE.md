# Meridian Voice — Make It a Powerhouse for the Entrepreneur Demo

**Goal:** In a few hours, position Meridian Voice as the greatest thing to touch the undergrad job market. One clear idea: **the career coach who's already read your resume—in their pocket, 24/7.**

---

## How to frame it (for you + the entrepreneur)

- **Sound bite:** "Meridian Voice is the career consultant every undergrad deserves but most never get. It already knows their resume, their track, and their scores—and it talks like a real advisor, not a bot. Career centers are stretched; we're 24/7."
- **Why it's a powerhouse:** Students get 10 minutes with a counselor once a semester. Voice is instant, personalized, and always on. It's the unfair advantage: the kid with Voice is prepped before every application and every interview.

---

## Quick wins (implement before the meeting)

1. **Personalized empty state** — When they open Voice, the first thing they see uses their name/major/track: "Hey [Name], I've got your profile—[major], [track]. Ask me anything about your resume, your scores, or your next move." Makes it obvious Voice isn't generic; it's *their* consultant.
2. **Punchy tagline** — Replace "Your career consultant—warm, real advice whenever you need it" with something that sells: **"Your career coach. Already read your resume."** or **"The advisor who already knows your story."**
3. **Hero suggestion** — Add one suggestion that wows: **"What's the one thing I should do this week to stand out?"** or **"Why did Meridian score me this way—in plain English?"** Put it first in the "Try asking" list so it's the first chip they see.
4. **"What can you do?" response** — When someone asks what Voice can help with, the model should give a tight, impressive 2–3 sentence pitch (resume, scores, interviews, next steps) and invite one specific question. Add a line to the system prompt so meta-questions about Voice get a killer answer.

---

## Demo script (30 seconds)

1. **Open the app** → Go to Meridian Voice.
2. **Point at the screen:** "This is Meridian Voice. It's like having a career coach who's already read your resume. See—it knows my major, my track, and my last Meridian scores."
3. **Ask one question** — Use the hero suggestion or: "What's the one thing I should do this week to stand out?" Let them see the reply: personalized, warm, actionable.
4. **Point at follow-ups:** "And it suggests what to ask next—so students never hit a dead end."
5. **Close:** "For undergrads who can't get enough time with their career center, this is the unfair advantage. We're starting at Tampa; this scales to every campus."

---

## Longer-term (after the meeting)

- **"Prep me for this role"** — User pastes a job title or description; Voice tailors advice (interview prep, resume tweaks).
- **Voice input** — Literal voice: talk to your coach. Huge differentiator.
- **Weekly nudge** — "One thing to do this week" pushed to the student based on their profile and last audit.
- **Career center dashboard** — Advisors see anonymized "what students are asking Voice" so they know where the gaps are.
