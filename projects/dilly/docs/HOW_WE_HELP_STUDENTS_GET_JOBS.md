# How Meridian Can Significantly Help Students Get Jobs

**Goal:** From “we parse and audit your resume” to “we help you get hired.” These are concrete ways to close that gap.

---

## What Already Helps (Resume → Stronger Candidacy)

- **Parse + structure** — Resume becomes a consistent text file (Name, Contact, Education, Experience, etc.) so nothing is “invisible” to recruiters or ATS.
- **Smart / Grit / Build scores** — Students see where they’re strong and weak by dimension; recommendations are tied to low scores so they know what to fix first.
- **Track-specific advice** — Pre-Health, Tech, Pre-Law, Business, etc. each get advice that matches what hiring committees and recruiters in that field actually look for.
- **Copy-paste improvements** — “Current line” vs “Suggested line” + Copy button so they can apply edits without guessing.
- **Progress (re-audit)** — “Last time vs this time” + “Why your scores changed” so they see that their edits worked.
- **PDF report** — Shareable, professional-looking deliverable for career center or mentors.
- **Red flags & consistency** — Recruiter turn-offs and duplicate/misattributed facts get called out so they fix mistakes before applying.

---

## Ways to Help More (Prioritized by Impact)

### 1. Make “fixing” the resume feel doable (high impact)

- **Live in-app resume editor** — Edit sections and bullets inside Meridian; re-audit with one click. No round-trip to Word/PDF. (Already in IDEAS #1.)
- **“What are you applying to?”** — Internship vs full-time vs grad school changes what we emphasize (e.g. projects vs tenure, recency). Tailor recommendations to that goal. (IDEAS #2, on roadmap.)
- **1-page nudge** — Detect when the resume is over one page and suggest trims (weaker bullets, optional sections) so they get to one page without losing impact. (IDEAS #9.)
- **Prioritized to‑do** — One clear “Do these 3 things next” derived from findings + recommendations so they’re not overwhelmed.

### 2. Turn the resume into applications (high impact)

- **Job / internship targeting** — Optional “I’m applying to: [role type / industry]” so recommendations and language align with that target (e.g. “tailor for consulting” vs “tailor for lab tech”).
- **ATS-friendly checklist** — After audit: “Your resume is ready for ATS” vs “Add keywords from the job description” + simple keyword-gap nudge (e.g. from a pasted job description).
- **Application tracker (lightweight)** — “Where did you apply?” — link report or score to company/role so they (or career center) see volume and follow-up.

### 3. Prove impact and build habit (medium impact)

- **Shareable badge** — “Meridian 85” or “100 Grit” for LinkedIn/profile. Social proof + reminder to keep resume updated. (IDEAS #10, on roadmap.)
- **Cohort + benchmark on dashboard** — “Your Grit vs Tech cohort” so they see how they compare and what “good” looks like for their track.
- **Gentle GPA nudge** — When GPA is missing, use signals (honors, Dean’s List, or lack of academic highlights) to suggest adding it if it’s strong, or to strengthen other dimensions. No invented numbers. (IDEAS #11.)

### 4. Beyond the resume (medium / later)

- **Workshops in the app** — Short, track-relevant workshops (e.g. “How to get 500+ LinkedIn connections,” “First technical interview”). Increases stickiness and perceived value. (IDEAS #3.)
- **Free certifications hub** — Curated free certs (by track) so they can “fill Build” with real credentials. (IDEAS #4.)
- **Mentors / JobBook** — Mentor directory or internship/job list filtered by major so the app is a single place for “fix resume” + “find opportunity.” (IDEAS #7, #8.)

### 5. Campus and career center (scale)

- **Batch audit + cohort report** — Already there: career center uploads resumes, gets averages and distribution by track. Use for workshops (“this cohort’s average Grit is 72”) and targeting.
- **Per-student report links** — Career center sends a link; student sees their report without logging in. (IDEAS, on hold.)
- **Recruiter view (later)** — Recruiter uploads a job description and sees “Meridian-fit” attributes or anonymized score bands for a candidate pool.

---

## Quick Wins (Low Effort, Clear Benefit)

- **“Do these 3 next”** — From recommendations + findings, surface exactly 3 actions (e.g. “Add dates to Experience,” “Rewrite this bullet,” “Add one quantifiable result”).
- **Cohort definitions on results** — One line under the radar: “For [Tech]: Smart = academic + technical rigor, Grit = ownership + impact, Build = projects + stack.”
- **Copy share link + “Send to career advisor”** — Same 7-day link, but CTA: “Email this link to your advisor.”

---

## Summary

**Today:** Parse → audit → scores + findings + recommendations + PDF + progress. That already helps students “fix” the resume.

**Next:** Make fixing easier (in-app edit, 1-page nudge, “what are you applying to?”), then connect the resume to applications (target role, ATS nudge, optional application link). After that: proof and habit (badges, cohort benchmark, GPA nudge) and beyond-resume value (workshops, certs, mentors/JobBook). That’s how the app can **significantly** help students get jobs—by being the place where they improve the resume, see progress, and connect it to real applications and opportunities.
