# How to say what Meridian is (without underselling it)

**Use this when someone asks "What is this?" or "What do you do?" You want to sound clear, confident, and like it's worth something.**

---

## Intro → Hook → Explanation (full script)

Use this when you have the floor—e.g. at the start of a meeting or when they say "So what are you working on?"

### Intro (who + what you're here for)

*"I'm [name]. I'm building Meridian—it's for college students who are applying to internships and jobs. I'm here to get your take on the product and see if there are people in your world who'd benefit from seeing it."*

Or shorter: *"I'm [name]. I built Meridian, a career app for undergrads. I'd love your feedback and any intros you think could help."*

---

### Hook (why they should care—one line)

*"Recruiters spend seconds on a resume and students get one shot. We're the bar they run against before they hit submit—so they don't blow it."*

Or: *"Every undergrad applying to jobs needs to be ready. We're the last check before they apply."*

Or: *"Career centers can't scale. We're the career coach in their pocket—24/7."*

Pick one. Say it. Pause.

---

### Explanation (what it is + why it matters)

*"We score their resume the way a senior hiring manager would—on three dimensions we call Smart, Grit, and Build. We tell them exactly what to fix, with concrete copy-paste suggestions and a shareable report, and it's all tailored to their field—Pre-Health, Tech, Pre-Law, whatever they're in. We also have a chat so they can ask career questions anytime. We're starting with University of Tampa, we're .edu-only, and we don't sell their data to recruiters. So when they apply, they're not guessing—they've already run the gauntlet with us."*

**Shorter version:**  
*"We score their resume like a hiring manager would, give them the edits and a report by track, and a chat for questions. We're live at Tampa, .edu-only, no selling data. They run the gauntlet with us before they apply."*

---

**Order:** Intro (who + why you're here) → Hook (one punchy reason to care) → Explanation (what it is and why it matters). Then stop and let them react.

## Career Center: what's there now + what's coming

When they ask "What else does it do?" or "Where is this going?"—use this so it doesn't sound like one feature.

**Today in the Career Center:** Resume audit (Smart, Grit, Build, red flags, copy-paste suggestions), your numbers and progress over time, track-specific playbooks, Meridian Voice (career Q&A 24/7), shareable PDF report and one-line summary, peer percentiles ("Top X% in your track").

**Coming—so we're the full career center, not just a resume tool:** We're adding the things students get from Handshake, LinkedIn, and tools like Quinncia, but in one place and without selling their data to recruiters:

- **Interview prep** — Scripts and talking points from *their* resume evidence (like Quinncia's mock interviews, but grounded in their Meridian scores).
- **Mock audit for a job** — Paste a job description; we score their resume against that role so they know if they're ready (reduces "am I wasting my time applying?").
- **Internships & jobs** — A place to discover roles by major/track (JobBook-style), positioned as easier than Handshake for *finding* while we own *getting ready*.
- **Workshops & resources** — Short, track-relevant workshops and tips in-app (interview prep, LinkedIn, "when to add GPA") so they don't have to wait for career center office hours.
- **Shareable badge** — "Meridian 85" or "100 Grit" for LinkedIn so they can show they've run the gauntlet.
- **Goals & deadlines** — Set a target (e.g. "Summer analyst at Goldman"); we show countdown and a sprint plan so they use time left.
- **Mentors** — Optional directory or matching so they can reach out to Meridian-affiliated mentors.

**One line you can say:** *"Right now it's resume audit, Voice, and their progress. We're building out interview prep, job discovery, workshops, and a shareable badge—so it's the career center in their pocket, not just one tool. And we don't sell their data to recruiters."*

## The one sentence (when you have 10 seconds)

Pick one and say it like you mean it:

- **"Meridian is the last check before students apply—we score their resume the way a senior hiring manager would and tell them exactly what to fix so they don't blow their one shot."**
- **"We're the career coach in their pocket: we've already read their resume, we score them on what actually gets them hired, and we tell them what to change before they hit submit."**
- **"It's the career center every undergrad deserves but most never get—24/7, personalized to their track, and built so they show up ready when recruiters spend six seconds on their resume."**

Say it once. Pause. Let them react. Don't rush to add more.

---

## The two sentences (when they say "Tell me more")

**Sentence 1 — What it is:**  
"We score students' resumes on the three things that actually get them hired—we call them Smart, Grit, and Build—and we give them concrete edits and a shareable report, tailored to their field—Pre-Health, Tech, Pre-Law, whatever."

**Sentence 2 — Why it matters:**  
"Recruiters spend seconds on a resume and career centers are stretched. We're the bar they need before they apply, plus a chat so they can ask questions anytime. We're starting with University of Tampa and we don't sell their data to recruiters."

That's it. You've said what it is, why it's valuable, and that you're real (one school, clear promise).

---

## Why it's worth something (if you need the logic in your head)

- **The problem:** Students apply with resumes that aren't ready. They get one shot. Career centers can't scale. Handshake and LinkedIn make them feel like a number.
- **What we do:** We hold them to the bar a senior hiring manager would—same criteria, same language—and we tell them exactly what to fix, with copy-paste suggestions and a report they can share.
- **The outcome:** They show up stronger. They don't waste applications. Schools get a force multiplier for their career office. We're not "just feedback"—we're the standard they run against before they submit.

So when you say "we score resumes," you're really saying: **we're the quality gate before they use their one shot.** That's worth something.

---

## What NOT to say (phrases that make it sound small)

| Avoid | Say instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "It's just a resume checker" | "It's the last check before they apply—like having a hiring manager in their pocket." |
| "We give feedback on resumes" | "We score them the way recruiters do and tell them exactly what to fix." |
| "It's for students" (vague) | "It's for undergrads who are applying to internships and jobs—we get them ready before they hit submit." |
| "We're still small" / "We're just starting" (unless they ask) | Lead with what it does and who it's for. You can say "We're starting with University of Tampa" as proof you're real, not as an apology. |
| "It's like Grammarly but for resumes" | "It's like having a senior hiring manager score your resume and tell you what to change—by track, with concrete edits." |
| "We're building an app that..." | "We built an app that..." (past tense = it exists and works.) |

**Rule:** Lead with the **outcome** (they don't blow their shot; they show up ready) and the **gap** (career centers can't scale; we can). Don't lead with "we have a feature."

## If they push back

**"So it's just a resume checker?"**  
"It's the bar they run against before they apply. We score them the way a hiring manager would—Smart, Grit, Build—and we give them the edits and the report. So when they submit, they're not guessing. Career centers love it because they can't do 24/7 one-on-one; we can."

**"Why would students pay?"**  
"Same reason they pay for anything that gets them hired—we save them from wasting applications and from showing up weak. It's less than a coffee a month. We're also talking to schools about making it part of the career center so students get it as a benefit."

**"What's different from Handshake?"**  
"Handshake is where they apply. We're what they use *before* they apply—to get their resume and their story right. We don't sell their data to recruiters. We're the coach; Handshake is the field."

**"How big is this?"**  
"Every undergrad applying to internships and jobs needs to be ready. We're starting with one campus, then we scale. The product works; now it's distribution and partnerships."

---

## Practice

1. Say the one sentence out loud. Then the two sentences. No filler.
2. If you hear yourself say "just" or "only" or "it's basically," stop and use the table above.
3. When in doubt, end with: **"We're the thing that gets them ready before they apply. That's Meridian."**

You've built something real. Say it like you believe it.
