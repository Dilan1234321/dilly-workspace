   - Use in sales, career center pitches, and investor conversations.

3. **Career center pitch**
   - "Quinncia does mock interviews. Meridian does resume scoring, job-fit, ATS, and interview prep—all in one place, with track-specific advice. We're the career center in their pocket."

### B. Add Video Mock Interviews (The One Gap That Matters)

**Goal:** Match Quinncia's video mock interview capability, then differentiate.

1. **Video mock interview module**
   - User records answers to AI-generated questions (from resume + track).
   - Analyze: eye contact, enthusiasm, background, lighting.
   - Optional: speech rate, filler words (audio analysis).
   - Score and badge (Bronze/Silver/Gold or Meridian tiers).

2. **Differentiation**
   - Questions grounded in *our* scores and evidence.
   - "We ask about your weakest dimension."
   - "We focus on the gaps in your profile."
   - Tie interview feedback back to Smart/Grit/Build.

3. **Integration**
   - Interview prep flows from audit → "Am I Ready?" → mock interview.
   - One journey. Quinncia's resume and interview feel disconnected.

### C. Own the Full Journey

**Resume → Job-fit → Interview**

| Step | Meridian | Quinncia |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1. Score resume | Smart/Grit/Build, track-specific | Generic feedback |
| 2. "Am I ready for X?" | Am I Ready? + ATS check | — |
| 3. Interview prep | From evidence, by dimension | Mock interview (video) |
| 4. Practice | Text today; video (roadmap) | Video |

**Pitch:** "We're the full career center. Resume, job-fit, interview—one flow. Quinncia does pieces; we do the journey."

### D. Features Quinncia Doesn't Have (and Won't Soon)

1. **Proof layer**
   - Cross-check resume vs GitHub, LinkedIn, certs.

   - Quinncia has nothing like this.

2. **Recruiter view**
   - Recruiter uploads JD → sees Meridian-fit attributes.
   - Two-sided value: students get ready, recruiters get better candidates.

4. **Goals and deadlines**

## Part 7: ATS Mastery — The Full Competitive Breakdown

**Updated:** March 2026

### The Landscape

Three tools matter for ATS: **Quinncia** (campus), **Jobscan** (pro market, $50/mo), and **us**. Nobody else does real per-vendor simulation.

### How Meridian's Per-ATS Vendor Simulation Works

We simulate how **Workday, Greenhouse, iCIMS, and Lever** would each parse the same resume. This isn't re-skinning one score — each vendor has researched penalty weights based on their real parsing behavior:

1. **Signal extraction:** We run our full ATS analysis (issues, checklist, extraction, sections) and extract 40+ signals: structural (multi-column, tables, headers), content (bullets, verbs, quantification), dates (consistency, completeness), contact fields, experience/education extraction quality, skills count.

2. **Vendor-specific scoring:** Each vendor applies different weights:
   - **Workday** (-20 for multi-column, -18 for tables, -12 for missing dates) + knockout question awareness (auto-calculates experience years from dates)
   - **Greenhouse** (-10 for multi-column, -5 for content issues) + scorecard tips (content quality matters most here because interviewers use the parsed resume)
   - **iCIMS** (-16 for non-standard headers — their biggest weakness) + Textkernel NLP pipeline knowledge (skills taxonomy, semantic matching, indefinite profile storage)
   - **Lever** (-6 for multi-column, lenient on everything) + context inference capability

3. **Company-to-ATS lookup:** Student types "Amazon" → we auto-identify Workday → highlight that vendor's score and tips. Database of 100+ companies with fuzzy matching and alias support.

4. **Per-vendor output:** Score (0-100), pass/risky/fail verdict, what breaks (vendor-specific), what parses cleanly, vendor-specific tips with severity, companies using that ATS.

### Meridian vs Quinncia on ATS

| Feature | Meridian | Quinncia |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Per-vendor simulation | Workday, Greenhouse, iCIMS, Lever — each with separate score and tips | Claims "based on four ATS" but never names them, never shows per-vendor scores |
| Company-to-ATS lookup | "Amazon uses Workday, your score there is 72" | Nothing |
| What ATS sees | Full extraction simulation: name, email, phone, university, GPA, experience entries, skills | Black box |
| "Meridian reads" | "ATS can't read this, but I can — here's the fix" | "ATS couldn't figure this out" (stop there) |
| Keyword density + placement | Contextual vs bare, per-keyword placement map, JD match % | No keyword placement analysis |
| Parseability checklist | 13-point auto-detected checklist | Generic formatting checks |
| Transparency | Students see exactly why each score is what it is | Opaque scoring |

### Meridian vs Jobscan on ATS

| Feature | Meridian | Jobscan ($50/mo) |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Per-vendor simulation | 4 vendors with deep quirks | Claims vendor simulation but unclear depth |
| Company-to-ATS lookup | Built-in with 100+ companies | Has company-to-ATS detection |
| Price | $9.99/mo with full suite | $50/mo for ATS features alone |
| Target audience | Students (track-specific, peer benchmarking) | Working professionals |
| Resume scoring | Smart/Grit/Build + ATS + vendor sim | ATS match score only |
| "Meridian reads" interpretation | Unique — nobody else does this | No interpretation layer |
| Full career suite | Resume + job-fit + interview + voice + calendar | Resume + ATS + cover letter |

### Where We Now Excel

1. **Transparency nobody else has.** We show what ATS extracts, what breaks per vendor, what works, and why. Quinncia's black box and Jobscan's generic advice can't match this.

2. **Company-to-ATS lookup changes everything.** "I'm applying to Amazon" → "Amazon uses Workday, your score is 72, here's what to fix" is the single most useful sentence in ATS tools. Quinncia doesn't do it. Jobscan does, but at $50/mo.

3. **"Meridian reads" is our moat.** No other tool says "ATS can't read this, but I can, here's the fix." Quinncia stops at "ATS couldn't figure this out." We go further.

4. **Keyword density + placement is deeper than keyword matching.** We don't just say "you're missing Python." We say "Python appears in your skills list (0.4x weight) but not in any experience bullet (1.0x weight). Add it to a bullet." Nobody else shows placement quality.

5. **Price + breadth.** $9.99/mo for ATS + resume scoring + voice + interview + job-fit vs $50/mo for just ATS. For students, this is a no-brainer.

### What We Still Need (Honest Gaps)

1. **More companies in the lookup database.** We have 100+, need 500+. Can be expanded through public careers page analysis.
2. **Real-world validation.** We need students to test vendor sim against actual application outcomes. Are our Workday scores predictive? We need data.
3. **"Fix it for me" rewrites.** We tell students what's wrong per vendor, but we don't yet rewrite their bullets for them. Next feature to build.
