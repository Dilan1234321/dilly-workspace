# Meridian — Pitch Sykes UTampa
## 5 minutes spoken, no slides. 10 min Q&A after.

---

## THE PITCH (5 min)

### Hook (30 sec)

"Raise your hand if you've ever submitted a resume and had no idea if it was good enough.

Yeah. Every hand.

Here's what nobody tells you: the person reading your resume — the recruiter, the admissions committee, the hiring manager — they spend six to eight seconds on it. Six seconds. And in those six seconds, they're not reading your bullet points. They're pattern-matching. Does this person look like someone who succeeds in this role?

And you — the student — have no way to know what that pattern looks like. Until now."

### Problem (60 sec)

"Right now, 20 million undergraduates in the United States are applying for internships, jobs, and grad school with no real feedback on their resume. Think about that. The single most important document in their early career — and they're guessing.

Your university career center? Open 9 to 5, booked two weeks out, and the advisor giving you feedback probably hasn't recruited for your specific field in years. They'll tell you to use action verbs and keep it to one page. That's it.

So students turn to their friends, to ChatGPT, to Reddit threads. And what do they get? Generic advice. 'Looks good to me.' 'Maybe add more numbers.' Nobody tells them *where they actually stand* relative to the people they're competing against.

This is the gap. Not a lack of resume templates — a lack of truth."

### Solution (90 sec)

"Meridian is an AI career center that scores your resume the way a senior hiring manager actually reads it.

You upload your resume. Meridian reads every line and scores you on three dimensions: Smart — your academic rigor. Grit — your leadership and impact. Build — your proof that you can do the job.

But here's what makes this different from ChatGPT or Grammarly or any other tool: Meridian is track-specific. If you're pre-med, we score you the way medical school admissions committees read resumes. If you're in finance, we score you the way Big Four recruiters do. If you're in tech, we look for shipped projects and stack depth. Twelve different tracks, twelve different scoring frameworks.

And we don't make anything up. Every score is tied to evidence — a direct quote from your resume. We call it the Meridian Truth Standard. If it's not on your page, it doesn't count. No flattery, no hallucination.

After your scores, you get consultant-level recommendations. Not 'add more action verbs' — specific line edits. 'This bullet says you managed a team. Change it to: Led a 6-person team that increased event attendance by 40%.' We tell you exactly what to change and why it moves the needle.

And then there's Meridian Voice — a career coach that already knows your resume, your scores, your track, and your goals. Ask it anything, anytime. 'What should I do this week to stand out?' 'How do I explain this gap in my experience?' It's your career center, open 24/7, in your pocket."

### Proof (30 sec)

"This isn't a concept. Meridian is built and running. I built it — here at UTampa. The scoring engine works. The AI auditor works. The career coach works. We've audited real student resumes and the feedback has been the same every time: 'I didn't know that about my resume.' That moment — when a student sees their actual number and realizes what they need to fix — that's worth more than any career fair."

### Business (30 sec)

"The model is simple. $9.99 a month. Unlimited audits, PDF reports, peer benchmarking, and Voice. For context — students pay $16 a month for Chegg to cheat on homework. We're asking for $10 to actually get hired.

The market is 20 million U.S. undergraduates. If we capture 1% of that at $10 a month, that's $24 million a year in recurring revenue. And we start where we are — right here at UTampa — and expand school by school."

### Vision (30 sec)

"Career centers haven't changed in 30 years. They still operate on walk-in appointments and one-size-fits-all advice. Handshake and LinkedIn help you find jobs — but they don't tell you if you're ready. Nobody closes that loop.

Meridian does. We don't replace career centers — we make them irrelevant by being better, faster, and always available. Every student, every track, every school."

### Close (30 sec)

"Six seconds. That's what you get. Meridian makes sure that when those six seconds happen, your resume tells the right story.

Thank you."

## Q&A PREP (10 min)

Anticipate these. Practice answers out loud once.

**"How is this different from ChatGPT?"**
"ChatGPT gives generic resume advice. It doesn't know what Pre-Health admissions committees look for versus what consulting recruiters look for. It doesn't have a scoring framework. It doesn't benchmark you against peers in your track. And critically — it makes things up. Meridian only scores what's actually on your resume. That's the Truth Standard."

**"How do you know the scoring is accurate?"**
"The scoring framework is built on how actual recruiters and admissions committees evaluate candidates — academic rigor, quantifiable impact, and track-specific proof. We've validated it against real resumes and real outcomes. And because every score is tied to cited evidence from the resume, you can verify it yourself."

**"Why would students pay when career centers are free?"**
"Career centers are free and students still don't use them — because they're slow, generic, and inconvenient. Meridian gives you better feedback in 60 seconds than a 30-minute appointment you had to book two weeks ago. The question isn't whether students will pay $10 — it's whether they'll keep paying. And they will, because every time they update their resume or apply somewhere new, they need to re-audit."

**"How do you acquire users?"**
"We start at UTampa. Every student who gets their score wants to share it — 'Top 15% Grit in Pre-Health.' That's inherently viral on a campus. We expand school by school, starting with the schools where career centers are weakest and student ambition is highest."

**"What's your competitive moat?"**
"Three things. First, the scoring framework — Smart, Grit, Build is proprietary and track-specific. Second, the Truth Standard — we never hallucinate, which builds trust. Third, data. Every audit makes our benchmarks more accurate. The more students use Meridian, the more valuable the peer comparison becomes. That's a network effect."

**"What stage are you at?"**
"Built and functional. Not a mockup, not a pitch deck — a working product. Scoring engine, AI auditor, career coach, PDF reports, peer benchmarking. Ready for beta users."

**"What do you need?"**
"Beta testers and early believers. We're launching a free testing period at UTampa to validate retention and gather data. After that, we scale."

## DELIVERY TIPS

- **Pace yourself.** 5 minutes feels short until you're up there. Don't rush the hook.
- **Pause after "Until now."** Let it land.
- **The "raise your hand" opening works because it's participatory.** If the room is small and nobody raises their hand, pivot to: "Be honest — everyone in this room has done it."
- **Numbers land harder when you slow down.** "Six. Seconds." Not "sixseconds."
- **In Q&A, take a breath before answering.** Shows confidence, not scrambling.
- **If they ask something you don't know, say: "That's a great question and I don't want to make something up — can I follow up with you after?" That IS the Truth Standard in action.**
