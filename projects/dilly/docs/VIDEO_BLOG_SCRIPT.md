# Meridian Video Blog Script — "The Scary Truth About Your First Job"

**Tone:** Urgent, direct, slightly ominous. You're the friend who found out something they need to know.  
**Length:** ~4-5 minutes when read at natural pace  
**Hook:** Open with a question or statement that makes them stop scrolling.

---

## [0:00 - 0:30] HOOK

**[Look directly at camera. Pause. Let it land.]**

You're about to graduate. Or you're a year away. Or two. And you've been told the same thing your whole life: get the degree, get the internship, get the job.

Here's what nobody's telling you.

**The job you're training for might not exist when you get there.**

**[Pause.]**

I'm not trying to scare you. I'm trying to wake you up. Because what's happening right now — in the next 12 to 24 months — is going to change everything about how college students get hired. And most of you are completely unprepared.

---

## [0:30 - 1:30] THE REALITY

Let me give you a number: **300 million.**

That's how many jobs Goldman Sachs says AI will affect globally by 2030. Not "create." *Affect.* As in: automate, replace, or fundamentally change.

And guess which jobs go first?

Not the CEO. Not the surgeon. Not the creative director.

**Entry-level jobs.**

Junior analyst. Associate copywriter. First-year accountant. Support engineer. Administrative coordinator. Data entry. Customer success. The jobs that used to be the on-ramp for every college graduate.

Companies that used to hire five junior analysts now hire two — and give them AI tools to do the work of five. The other three positions? They don't exist anymore.

**[Lean in.]**

You're competing for fewer spots. Against more people. And here's the worst part: **your resume doesn't even work anymore.**

---

## [1:30 - 2:30] THE RESUME IS DEAD

Think about it. Everyone uses ChatGPT to write their resume. Everyone uses AI to draft their cover letter. Everyone has the same polished, professional, perfectly formatted document.

So when you apply to a job — and 500 other people apply to the same job — what does the recruiter see?

**500 identical resumes.**

Same structure. Same tone. Same buzzwords. Same "strong communication skills" and "detail-oriented" and "team player." There's nothing to differentiate you. You're not a person anymore. You're a PDF in a pile of PDFs.

And the companies? They're not hiring humans to read them. They're using AI to screen you. AI screening AI-written resumes. The whole system is eating itself.

**[Pause. Let that sink in.]**

So you spray 200 applications. You hear back from three. Maybe. And you have no idea why. Was it your GPA? Your major? A keyword you missed? A formatting thing that broke in their system? You'll never know. You're just… ghosted.

That's not a bug. That's the new normal.

---

## [2:30 - 3:15] THE STAKES

Let me make this personal.

You've got student loans. Maybe 30, 40, 50 thousand dollars. Maybe more. You've spent four years — or you're about to spend four years — building toward a career. Your parents are asking when you're gonna get a real job. Your friends are posting their offer letters. And you're sending application number 47 into the void, hoping this one sticks.

**What happens if it doesn't?**

What happens when the entry-level jobs keep shrinking? When the bar keeps rising? When "2-3 years of experience" becomes the minimum for an *entry-level* role?

**[Lower your voice. More intimate.]**

This isn't doom-mongering. This is already happening. I see it. My friends see it. Recruiters see it. The companies that used to hire fresh grads are hiring fewer of them, and they're expecting more. They want proof. They want evidence. They want to know you can actually do the job — not just that you wrote a nice resume.

**The old playbook is broken. And most of you are still following it.**

---

## [3:15 - 4:15] THE PIVOT — THERE'S A WAY

Okay. I didn't come here just to scare you. I came here because there's actually something you can do about it.

The students who are going to win in this market aren't the ones with the best GPA or the most internships. They're the ones who **stand out when everyone looks the same.** The ones who can prove what they've done — not just claim it. The ones who know exactly where they fit and how to get in front of the right people.

That's why I built Meridian.

Meridian isn't another resume builder. It's not another job board. It's a career infrastructure platform built for exactly this moment. For students who need to compete when the rules have changed.

**Here's what it does:**

- It audits your resume against *real* employer criteria — not generic advice. Smart, Grit, Build. Three dimensions that actually matter to hiring managers.
- It simulates how the top four ATS systems — Workday, Greenhouse, iCIMS, Lever — will parse your resume. So you fix what breaks *before* you apply.
- It matches you to jobs where you actually fit. Not keyword matching. Real fit. Based on your profile, your track, your evidence.
- It gives you a verified profile — a six-second view that recruiters can actually trust. Because when everyone's resume is AI-written, the thing that matters is proof.

And there's an AI coach — Meridian Voice — that knows your full profile. Your scores, your gaps, your goals. It runs mock interviews. It preps you for specific companies. It tells you exactly what to fix and why.

**This is the tool I wish I had when I started.** And it's the tool you need before the market gets even tighter.

---

## [4:15 - 4:45] CTA

**[Direct. Confident.]**

If you're a college student — if you're about to graduate, or you're a year or two away — you can't afford to wait. The jobs are shrinking. The competition is fierce. And the resume you're polishing might not even get read.

Meridian is built for this. Built at UTampa. Built by someone who's living this problem right now.

**[Show the link or QR code if on screen.]**

Link in the description. First audit is free. See where you stand. See what recruiters actually see. And start building the profile that gets you hired — not the one that gets lost in the pile.

**[Pause. Smile. Slight shift to hope.]**

The future isn't hopeless. But it is different. And the students who adapt first are the ones who win.

Get in. Before it's too late.

---

## [4:45 - 5:00] OUTRO

**[Optional: softer, more personal.]**

I'm Dilan. I'm building Meridian because I'm tired of watching smart people get ghosted. If this hit — if any of this scared you or pissed you off or made you think — drop a comment. Share it with someone who needs to hear it. And go run your first audit.

I'll see you there.

## Production Notes

- **Pacing:** Slow down on the scary parts. Speed up slightly on the solution. Let silence do work — pause after "300 million," after "500 identical resumes," after "What happens if it doesn't?"
- **Energy:** Start intense. Dip into dread in the middle. Rise into urgency and hope at the end. You're not selling doom — you're selling the way out.
- **Visuals (if editing):** Consider B-roll of job application screens, AI imagery, graduation caps, empty inbox. Contrast with Meridian app screens during the solution section.
- **Thumbnail idea:** Your face, concerned or intense. Text overlay: "Your First Job Might Not Exist" or "300M Jobs. Are Yours Safe?"
- **Title options:**  
  - "The Scary Truth About Your First Job (AI Is Coming)"  
  - "Why Your Resume Doesn't Work Anymore"  
  - "300 Million Jobs. What That Means for You."
