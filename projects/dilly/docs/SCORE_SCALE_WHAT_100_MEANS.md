# What the 0–100 Scores Mean (and What 100 Means)

**Purpose:** Canonical definition so product, recruiter view, and copy stay aligned. Scores are designed around what jobs look for.

---

## Goal of the scores

Meridian scores are **based on what employers in that track look for**. We're not scoring "how good a human you are." We're scoring **how well your resume shows what hiring managers and programs in that field care about** (academic/rigor, leadership/impact, track-specific proof). So the scale is anchored to **job readiness and fit**, not an abstract "quality" scale.

---

## The scale (0–100)

- **0–100** is the range for each dimension (Smart, Grit, Build) and for the composite (final) score.
- **Track-specific:** The bar is set by what **that track's** top-tier recruiters and programs value (Tech vs Pre-Health vs Finance, etc.). Same number scale everywhere; what "counts" toward the score is track-specific.
- **Meridian Truth Standard:** We only use what's on the resume. We don't invent facts. So the score is "how well this resume, as written, signals what jobs look for."

---

## What 100 means

**100 = exceptional on this dimension for what employers in that track look for — at the top of our scale.**

So:

- **Smart 100:** The resume shows the kind of academic/technical rigor (education, coursework, honors, research, certifications) that top employers in that field look for, at the highest level we represent. We're not saying "perfect human"; we're saying "as strong as we represent on this dimension for this track."
- **Grit 100:** The resume shows the kind of leadership, quantifiable impact, and ownership that top employers in that field look for, at the highest level we represent.
- **Build 100:** The resume shows the kind of track-specific proof (projects, experience, portfolio, clinical hours, etc.) that top employers in that field look for, at the highest level we represent.

So **100 is the upper bound of the scale** — "what strong hires look like on paper for this dimension" at the top of our 0–100 range. It's relative to **what jobs look for**, not to a universal "perfect" score.

---

## How this ties to jobs

- **Student scores:** "Your resume shows X, Y, Z for Smart, Grit, Build — here's how that lines up with what employers in your track look for." Tiers (e.g. Strong 70+, Elite 85+) are calibrated to what we use for benchmarks and "Am I Ready?"-style fit.
- **Job bar (recruiter view / JD-fit):** "This role typically wants at least Smart X, Grit Y, Build Z." Those numbers sit on the **same scale**: 100 = exceptional for what that role/track looks for; 70 = solid; etc. So when we show an aspirational bar (e.g. 78 Smart for a role), we're saying "aim for this level of signal so you're a strong candidate."

**One-sentence definition for in-app or recruiter copy:**  
*"100 means exceptional on that dimension for what employers in your field look for — the top of our scale."*

---

## Expectations: scores vs interviews/jobs

**Risk:** If we say "100 = exceptional for what employers look for," students may assume "so I'll get interviews or jobs." That's an over-read. We need to be clear about what we can and can't deliver.

**What we can deliver:** We can help students improve their resume so it signals more of what employers look for (higher Smart, Grit, Build). We can get many students to strong scores (e.g. 70+, 85+) with recommendations and line edits. Reaching 100 is possible in principle but not promised. Improving toward the bar makes them a stronger candidate and increases the chance of interviews—we don't guarantee outcomes.

**What we cannot deliver:** We cannot guarantee interviews or job offers. Hiring depends on fit, timing, competition, and interview performance. A 100 means "exceptional on paper"; it does not mean "you will get the job."

**Framing for product/copy:** Scores = resume signal ("how well your resume signals what employers look for"). Goal = stronger candidate. Optional in-app line: "Strong scores mean you're signaling what employers look for. We can't guarantee interviews, but we're here to get your resume to that level." Never imply that 100 or any score guarantees interviews or jobs.
