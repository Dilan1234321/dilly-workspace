# Selling Meridian to University Career Centers

**Problem:** Career centers have been using Handshake, Quinncia, etc. for years. How do you get them to add (or switch to) Meridian?

---

## 1. Don’t compete with Handshake — sit next to it

- **Handshake** = jobs, employers, events, applications. Career centers won’t rip it out.
- **Meridian** = resume readiness, scoring, and “am I ready?” before they apply. Different job.
- **Pitch:** “We don’t replace Handshake. Students use Handshake to find jobs and apply. They use Meridian to get *ready* — so when they apply, they don’t waste their shot.”

Position Meridian as **complementary**: Handshake for *where* to apply, Meridian for *when* they’re ready and *what* to fix.

---

## 2. Quinncia is the one you’re replacing (or adding alongside)

- **Quinncia** = resume review + mock interviews. That’s where you overlap.
- Career centers that have Quinncia either:
  - **Add Meridian** for a pilot (e.g. one track or one cohort), or
  - **Switch** if they’re unhappy with Quinncia (generic advice, no track-specific, no “why,” no job-fit).
- **Pitch:** “If you’re happy with Quinncia for mock interviews, keep it. Use Meridian for resume scoring and readiness — track-specific, evidence-based, and we tell students exactly what to change and why. If you’re not happy with resume feedback, we’re a better resume engine.”

Use the differentiators in `HOW_WE_BLOW_QUINNCIA_OUT_OF_THE_WATER.md` (track-specific, copy-paste edits, progress over time, job-fit, ATS by vendor, Voice, no data sold).

---

## 3. Why a career center would say yes

| Their pain | How Meridian helps |
|------------|---------------------|
| Understaffed; can’t see every student | Meridian is 24/7. Students get scored and coached without an appointment. |
| Generic “use action verbs” advice | Track-specific: Pre-Health vs Tech vs Consulting get different criteria and playbooks. |
| No visibility into cohort readiness | Batch audit + cohort report: averages by track, distribution. Use in workshops and targeting. |
| Students don’t know if they’re ready to apply | “Am I Ready?” for company/role + job-fit + ATS. Reduces “am I wasting my time?” |
| Want better placement outcomes | Meridian improves resume quality and readiness before they hit Handshake. Better applications → better outcomes. |
| Students don’t trust “another tool” | .edu-only, we don’t sell data to recruiters. Position as student-first. |

Lead with **scale** (you can’t 1:1 everyone; Meridian scales) and **outcomes** (readiness before apply, track-specific, evidence-based).

---

## 4. How to get in the door

**A. Don’t lead with “replace everything.”**  
Lead with: “Add Meridian for resume readiness. Pilot with one track or one cohort (e.g. Pre-Health or Tech). No commitment to drop Handshake or Quinncia.”

**B. Create proof at one school first.**  
UTampa: get students using it, get a few “4 PA interviews after Meridian” stories, get the career center to co-brand or recommend it. Then: “Built with UTampa Career Center. Here’s what happened.”

**C. Let students pull.**  
If students on a campus are using Meridian (viral, word of mouth, student orgs), go to the career center with: “Your students are already using Meridian. Here’s a cohort view you could have if we partner.” They care about adoption; show it.

**D. Offer a real pilot.**  
“4–6 week pilot: we give you N seats (or campus-wide). You promote it to one track or one class. We give you a cohort report at the end — scores by track, improvement over time. No contract. If it works, we talk license.”

**E. Find one champion.**  
Director, associate director, or a dedicated advisor for Pre-Health/Tech who’s frustrated with generic tools. They can push for a pilot or a budget line.

**F. Use batch/cohort as the carrot.**  
You already have (or can highlight) batch audit + cohort report. “Upload your workshop’s resumes and get a single report: average Grit for Pre-Health, distribution, who’s on track.” That’s a clear “why buy” for workshops and assessment.

---

## 5. Objection handling

| “We already have Handshake.” | “We’re not replacing Handshake. Students use Handshake to apply. They use Meridian to get ready before they apply — so their resume and readiness are solid when they hit Handshake.” |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| “We have Quinncia.” | “Quinncia does resume review and mock interviews. Meridian is track-specific resume scoring plus job-fit and ATS — we tell students *why* and *how* to fix, with copy-paste edits and progress over time. Run a short pilot with one cohort and compare.” |
| “We don’t have budget.” | “Start with a free pilot: we give you seats, you promote to one track. You get cohort insights; we get proof. If outcomes improve, we talk about a license. No upfront cost.” |
| “Procurement takes forever.” | “Pilot doesn’t have to go through procurement — it’s a trial. Meanwhile, students can use Meridian individually (we’re already at UTampa). When the center is ready, we do a proper agreement.” |
| “Students won’t use another tool.” | “Meridian is the last check before they apply — they get a score and a report they can use in Handshake or at the career fair. We’re .edu-only and we don’t sell their data. Position it as *their* career coach, not another admin tool.” |

---

## 6. One-line pitches

- **For the first meeting:** “Meridian is resume readiness and a career coach in one — track-specific, evidence-based, 24/7. We don’t replace Handshake; we make students ready before they use it. Can we run a short pilot with one of your tracks?”
- **When they have Quinncia:** “Quinncia does mock interviews and resume feedback. Meridian does track-specific resume scoring, job-fit, and ATS — with clear ‘why’ and copy-paste fixes. Adding Meridian for resume readiness gives you both; or we can pilot and you compare.”
- **For the champion:** “Your students are under-served on resume feedback. Meridian scales what you’d do in a 1:1 — score, evidence, and next steps — by track. One pilot, one cohort, one report. No commitment.”

---

## 7. What you need to run this

1. **Pilot offer** — Clear “what we give, what they do, how long, what they get” (e.g. cohort report, N seats).
2. **Cohort report** — Batch audit output that a career center can use in workshops or reporting (you have or can expose this).
3. **Proof** — 1–2 UTampa (or other) stories: “X students used Meridian; here’s placement or improvement.”
4. **One-pager** — Meridian vs Quinncia (resume/readiness only), “why add Meridian,” pilot terms.
5. **Pricing** — So when they say “we’re interested,” you have a number (per-seat, per-year, or pilot → license).

---

**Summary:** Don’t fight Handshake. Position Meridian as “resume readiness + coach” that sits alongside it. For Quinncia, position as “add or replace for resume/readiness” with a low-friction pilot and proof. Use student adoption and cohort reporting to create pull from the center.
