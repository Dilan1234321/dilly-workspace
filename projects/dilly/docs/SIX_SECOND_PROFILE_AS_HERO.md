# Six-Second Profile: Make It the Revolution

**Goal:** Position the Six-second profile as Meridian's top selling point—something that feels *revolutionizing*, not just "a nice feature."

---

## The Insight (Why This Is Revolutionary)

**The brutal truth:** Recruiters spend 6–8 seconds on a resume. They're not reading—they're pattern-matching. "Does this person look like someone who succeeds in this role?"

**The gap:** Every other tool optimizes your *resume*—the document. But the document was never designed for a 6-second scan. It was designed to be *read*. Meridian does something nobody else does: **we give you a second screen.**

- **Resume** = the teaser (1 page, gets you in the door)
- **Six-second profile** = the trailer (1 link, 6 seconds, gets you the callback)

You're not replacing the resume. You're adding the layer that was always missing: **the page built for how recruiters actually read.**

---

## Positioning Frameworks

### 1. The Paradigm Shift
> "Your resume is a document. Your Meridian profile is a *performance*—designed for the 6-second scan. One link. One scan. Your full story."

### 2. The Secret Hack
> "Recruiters don't read. They scan. Your resume gets 6 seconds. We built the page that wins those 6 seconds. Add the link. That's the cheat code."

### 3. The Math
> "1 page. 6 seconds. 1 link. Your resume is the teaser. Your Meridian profile is what gets you the callback."

### 4. The Before/After
> **Before:** Your resume lands. Maybe they read it. Maybe they don't. You're guessing.  
> **After:** Your resume lands. They scan. They see the link. They click. In 6 seconds they know your scores, your proof, your story. They know you're ready.

### 5. The Credit Score Analogy
> "A credit score is one number that tells a whole story. Your Meridian profile is one page that tells a whole story. Both are trust signals that shortcut the evaluation."

### 6. The Outcome
> "Students who add the link get callbacks. The ones who don't are still hoping."

## What to Change (Product & UX)

### In the app
- **Elevate the "Add to your resume" block** — Make it the hero outcome, not a sidebar. It should feel like *the main reason* you ran an audit.
- **Rename it** — "Your recruiter profile" or "Your 6-second card" feels more revolutionary than "Add to your resume."
- **Show the value** — "This is what recruiters see when they click. One link. One scan. Your full story."
- **Add a preview** — Let users see the profile *as a recruiter would* before they add it. "This is your 6-second moment."

### On the website
- **Lead with it** — Consider making the six-second profile the hero of the homepage. "Your resume gets 6 seconds. We built the page that wins them."
- **Add a dedicated section** — "The Second Screen" or "Built for the 6-Second Scan" with a mockup of the profile.
- **Differentiate** — "LinkedIn gives you a profile. Meridian gives you a *recruiter profile*—built for the way they actually read."

### In the pitch
- **Close with it** — "Six seconds. That's what you get. Meridian gives you the page that wins them. One link at the bottom of your resume. That's the revolution."
- **Add a demo** — Show the profile live. "This is what recruiters see. In 6 seconds they know your scores, your proof, your story."
