The Career Center
Students Actually Deserve

Every university promises a career center. Most students never use theirs. The ones who do wait weeks for a 15-minute appointment that ends with "looks good!" We built Dilly because that's not good enough.

The problem

Career centers weren't built for this moment

There's one counselor for every 2,500 students. Appointments fill up weeks out. When you finally sit down, you get fifteen minutes of surface-level advice that could apply to anyone with a pulse and a PDF.

Meanwhile, AI is rewriting every industry in real time. Roles that existed last year are disappearing. New ones are appearing faster than any career center can track. Students are graduating into a job market their professors barely understand - and the institutions meant to prepare them are using the same playbook from 2008.

The students who land the best jobs aren't the ones with the best resumes. They're the ones with parents who know people, schools with resources, and advisors who pick up the phone. Everyone else gets a resume template and a "good luck."

That's not a career center. That's a checkbox.

What we built

A career center that knows your name

Dilly is what career services should have been all along: a personal advisor that knows your resume, understands the market, and is available whenever you need it - not in three weeks, not for fifteen minutes, not with generic advice.

Upload your resume and Dilly scores you on three dimensions: Smart (academics and rigor), Grit (leadership and impact), and Build (what you've actually shipped). Your score isn't a grade - it's a GPS. It tells you exactly where you are, exactly where to go, and exactly how to get there.

Then Dilly matches you to real internships and jobs - not a job board with ten thousand listings, but a curated feed of roles you're actually qualified for, with a clear breakdown of which ones you're ready for, which are within reach, and which need work.

The Score

Nobody else in the world tells a student "you are a 77." Dilly quantifies career readiness with a number - and a map to make it higher.

The Advisor

An AI that knows your resume, your scores, your goals, and the market. It gives real advice - not "looks great!" - at 2am or 2pm.

The Matches

Real jobs from real companies, scored against your specific profile. Ready, Almost, or Gap - you always know where you stand.

Why now

AI is changing everything. Students need a guide.

Every week there's a new headline: another company automating roles, another industry being reshaped, another set of skills becoming obsolete overnight. Students see this. Parents see this. The anxiety is real.

The students graduating today are entering a job market that moves faster than any career center, any professor, or any LinkedIn post can keep up with. They don't need more information - they're drowning in it. They need someone who understands them personally and can cut through the noise.

Dilly is that someone. It tracks the market in real time. It knows which skills employers are actually hiring for - not last year, not "in general," but right now, for roles you're qualified for. When the market shifts, your advice shifts with it.

For students, Dilly says: "The world is changing, but you're not lost. Here's where you stand. Here's what to do next. Here are the companies that want someone like you."

For parents, Dilly provides something just as important: peace of mind. Your kid isn't sending resumes into a void. They have a score, a plan, and an advisor that's always on - helping them navigate a world that's moving faster than any generation before them had to.

The founder

Dilan Kochhar

Founder & CEO

University of Tampa · Data Science · Mathematics · Computer Science

I watched friends with 3.8 GPAs and real internship experience get passed over - not because they weren't qualified, but because their resumes didn't tell the right story. I sat in the career center. One counselor, hundreds of students, three-week waitlist. The advice I got? "Your resume looks fine."

It wasn't fine. And "fine" doesn't get you an interview.

I built Dilly because I needed it. Then I realized every student I knew needed it too. Not another resume scanner - a system that actually understands who you are, what you've done, and what the market wants. One that doesn't close at 5pm and doesn't give you the same advice it gives everyone else.

We started at UTampa. We're not trying to be everything to everyone. We're building the best career tool one school has ever seen - and then we're doing it again at the next one, and the next one, until every student has access to the career center they were promised.
