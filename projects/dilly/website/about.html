The Career Center
Students Were Promised

Every university promises career support. Most students never get it. The ones who do wait weeks for a 15-minute appointment that ends with generic advice. We built Dilly because students deserve better.

The problem

Career centers are broken

The average university career center has 1 counselor for every 2,500 students. Appointments book out weeks in advance. When you finally get one, you get 15 minutes of surface-level advice that could apply to anyone.

Meanwhile, AI is reshaping every industry. Students are graduating into a job market that looks nothing like what their parents experienced. The roles that exist today might not exist in five years. The skills that matter are changing faster than any career center can keep up with.

Students don't need another resume template. They need someone who knows them, understands the market, and tells them exactly where they stand and what to do next.

The solution

A career center that knows you

Dilly gives every student what only the most connected students had before: a personal career advisor who knows your strengths, understands the market, and is available 24/7.

Upload your resume and Dilly scores you across three dimensions: Smart (your academics and technical knowledge), Grit (your leadership, initiative, and persistence), and Build (what you've actually created and shipped).

Your score isn't just a number. It's a map. Dilly breaks it down by career field, matches you to real jobs, tells you which ones you're ready for, and shows you exactly how to close the gaps on the ones you're not.

The founder

Dilan Kochhar

Founder & CEO

University of Tampa · Data Science · Mathematics · Computer Science

I built Dilly because I lived the problem. I watched friends with 3.8 GPAs and real internship experience get passed over because their resumes didn't tell the right story. I saw the career center — one overworked counselor for thousands of students — and thought, there has to be a better way.

AI isn't just changing how we work. It's changing what employers look for, which roles exist, and how fast the market moves. Students need a tool that moves as fast as the world around them. That's what Dilly is.

We started at the University of Tampa. We're not trying to be everything to everyone. We're building the best career tool one school has ever seen — and then doing it again at the next one.
