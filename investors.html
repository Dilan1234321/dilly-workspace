The Shift

The career stack wasn't built for this generation.

Every surface a student touches — the resume, the ATS, the career center, the application — was designed for a labor market that no longer exists. AI broke the old signals. Gen-Z never trusted them in the first place.

01

The career center is unstaffed.

One advisor for every 2,263 students. Appointments booked weeks out. Students graduate without meeting one.

02

The resume is six seconds and a filter.

75% of applications are rejected by an ATS before a human sees them. The survivors get a six-second skim. Nobody learns anything.

03

AI made the old scoring obsolete.

Every resume now reads like it was written by a consultant. GPT polish is table stakes. The signal has moved — from what's on the page to what the student actually knows, has built, and wants.

Dilly is not a resume editor. It is the first career platform built around a student — not a document.

Core Thesis
The Product

Three surfaces, one profile, zero scores.

Everything Dilly does is grounded in a living profile the student builds through conversation. No Smart/Grit/Build. No ranking. No leaderboards. Just fit, narrative, and momentum.

01 — Foundation

The Dilly Profile

A structured, growing record of who the student is: experiences, skills, goals, fears, strengths, gaps. Built from conversation, seeded from a resume, edited by the user. The profile is the product.

02 — Signal

Fit Narratives

For every job, Dilly writes the student a personal read: what they have that matches, what's missing, what to do about it. No percentages. No grades. Cached per user-job pair. ~$0.001 each.

How It Works

The profile grows every time the student shows up.

Three input paths, one extraction pipeline, one evergreen profile. Dilly never asks the student to "update" anything — it learns.

01 ▸

Resume seed

Student uploads a resume at onboarding. Dilly parses, extracts, and converts it into structured profile facts. The resume is the ignition, not the engine.

02 ▸

Conversation

Every chat runs a background extraction that pulls new facts into the profile — achievements, fears, preferences, patterns. The student never has to "save" anything.

03 ▸

Direct edit

Users can add, edit, or remove facts from the My Dilly page. The profile is theirs. We never sell it, rank it, or show it to a third party without consent.

↓ Then the profile powers everything.

Output

Tailored resumes, per ATS.

Dilly generates a resume formatted for the exact ATS the target job uses (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, SmartRecruiters, USAJobs). No more guessing.

Output

Fit narratives on every job.

Green / amber / red dots on job cards. Tap for the full read. ~9,800 real listings, .edu-gated, refreshed continuously.

Output

Interview practice, personal.

Questions drawn from the student's actual experiences and target cohort. Feedback loops back into the profile.

Output

Dilly Card + shareable profile.

A clean public surface recruiters can read in 30 seconds. 8 templates. Profile facts woven into a human narrative, not a CV.

Retention Architecture

The moat isn't the features. It's the return.

Career tools are graveyards — students sign up once and forget. We ship retention primitives as first-class product, engineered for daily and weekly return on a surface that otherwise sees a student twice a year.

Daily

Daily Pulse

A one-tap daily check-in. Streaks, micro-actions, low cognitive cost. Builds the habit that makes everything else work.

Weekly

Field Pulse

Weekly return anchor. The AI Arena reads the student's path and frames data through 18 advisor lenses — same job market, 18 different readings.

Callback

Dilly Remembered You

Callback-to-past-context retention. "Last month you said X — here's what changed." The product has a memory; the student feels known.

Celebrate

Wins Timeline

A running log of what the student did, not what they failed at. Applications, callbacks, offers, first days. Worth opening the app to add to.

The profile is the moat.

Every conversation, every edit, every uploaded resume deepens a structured record that is unique to the student and non-portable to a competitor. Switching cost compounds with time-in-product.

The ATS knowledge is the moat.

Per-ATS formatting rules are not public API surface — they're learned from thousands of live postings. The more jobs we ingest, the sharper the resume output, the harder we are to catch.

.edu gating is the moat.

No bot spam, no adult users pretending to be students, no employers sneaking in to scrape. The gate is the product's integrity and the foundation of every future recruiter relationship.

The positioning is the moat.

Every competitor is racing to be a better resume tool. We built the one category where the resume is a side effect. Reframing against a market is the hardest moat to cross.
