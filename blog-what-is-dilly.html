What Is Dilly? The AI Career App for College Students Explained

Dilly is an AI-powered career platform built specifically for college students. It does something that no general job board or resume tool does: it tells you exactly where you stand relative to other students competing for the same internships and jobs.

If you've ever wondered why you're applying to dozens of internships and hearing nothing, or whether your resume is actually competitive in your field, Dilly was built to answer those questions with data instead of guesswork.

The Core Idea Behind Dilly

Most career tools treat every user the same. They give generic advice, run a keyword match against one ATS, and tell you to "quantify your impact." That's not wrong, but it's not useful either — because a finance student competing for Goldman Sachs summer analyst roles faces completely different competition than a nursing student applying to clinical rotations.

Dilly's premise is that career readiness is specific to your field, your school, and the roles you're targeting. A 3.6 GPA in a pre-med track means something different than a 3.6 in communications. A semester of research experience matters enormously in some tracks and not at all in others. Dilly accounts for this by benchmarking your profile against students in your specific career track across 43 different paths — from tech and finance to pre-health, education, and the arts.

How Dilly Scores Your Resume

The core of Dilly is a three-axis scoring system called Smart, Grit, and Build. Instead of giving you one number that hides everything, Dilly separates your profile into three dimensions that recruiters actually care about:

  • Smart measures academic signal — your GPA, coursework rigor, certifications, academic awards, and other evidence that you can handle the intellectual demands of a role.
  • Grit measures ownership and persistence — leadership roles, sustained commitments, jobs held during school, initiative on projects, and evidence that you push through rather than quit.
  • Build measures execution and proof — internships, shipped projects, research output, portfolios, clinical hours, or whatever counts as "done work" in your specific field.

Each score runs from 0 to 100 and is weighted differently depending on your track. A tech student's Build score weights shipped code and side projects heavily. A pre-law student's Build score weights legal research, moot court, and writing samples.

Dilly breaks your resume into three scores: Smart (academic strength), Grit (persistence), and Build (proof of work).

ATS Scanning Across 6 Systems

Dilly also runs your resume through six different ATS parsers — Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS, Taleo, and Ashby — and shows you what each one actually extracts from your document.

This matters because most resume "ATS checkers" test against a single system. But in practice, different companies use different ATS software, and a resume that parses perfectly in one system can break completely in another. Columns, tables, text boxes, headers, and certain fonts all cause parsing failures in some systems but not others.

Dilly shows you the raw text each parser pulled from your resume so you can see exactly what got lost, garbled, or misread — before it costs you an interview.

Dilly tests your resume against Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS, Taleo, and Ashby simultaneously.

Peer Benchmarking

Knowing your absolute score is less useful than knowing where you rank. Dilly shows your cohort percentile across Smart, Grit, and Build so you can see how you compare to other students in your major and career track.

If you're in the top 15% on Smart but the bottom 40% on Build in your track, that tells you something specific: you need more execution proof on your resume, not more coursework. That's the kind of directional signal that changes how you spend your time before recruiting season opens.

Who Is Dilly For?

Dilly requires a verified .edu email address. It's built for current college students — not recent grads, not professionals, not employers. The student gate is intentional. It keeps the benchmarks accurate and the community competitive in a productive way.

Within that, Dilly is most useful for students who:

  • Are applying to internships or entry-level roles and not getting responses
  • Want to know how competitive their profile actually is before a specific recruiting cycle opens
  • Are wondering whether their resume passes ATS filtering
  • Want to track multiple job applications in one place
  • Want specific, data-grounded feedback rather than generic resume tips

Is Dilly Free?

Yes — there is a real free tier that includes your Smart, Grit, and Build scores, a basic ATS check, access to the internship listings, and an application tracker. The free tier gives you enough to understand where you stand and what the biggest gaps are.

The paid tier (Dilly Pro) adds job-tailored resume rewrites, deep per-bullet scoring, full six-vendor ATS simulation with parse previews and fixes, cohort percentiles, per-listing readiness analysis, AI coaching, mock interview prep, and the full calendar and pipeline stack.

The free tier is a genuine diagnostic, not a teaser. You can use it indefinitely.
