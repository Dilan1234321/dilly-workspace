## Part 1 — The Largest Selling Points (The "Why")

*From advisor. These are the core differentiators.*

### 1. The "Anti-Prestige" Filter

**The problem:** In high-stakes hiring (Finance, Med, Tech), institutional bias is the norm. Recruiters default to Ivy League names because they lack a better proxy for quality.

**Why it sells:** Meridian turns a University of Tampa student’s **Grit** (e.g. balancing 20 clinical hours with a 3.8 GPA) into a **quantifiable metric** that can beat a "legacy" student with a 4.0 and no work history.

**Expansion idea:** Market to **mid-market firms** (e.g. Raymond James, boutique law firms) as a **"Hidden Gem" finder**. Pitch: *"Stop fighting for the same 5 Harvard grads; use Meridian to find the top 1% of Grit leaders in Florida."*

---

### 2. The 1.40x Logic Multiplier (Rigor Calibration)

**The insight:** A 4.0 in "General Studies" is not the same as a 3.5 in "Organic Chemistry" or "Data Structures."

**Why it sells:** It **restores the value of difficulty**. Students are encouraged to take hard classes because the **Meridian Smart Score** rewards rigor even when raw GPA is slightly lower.

**Expansion idea:** Create a **"Rigor Index" API**. Other companies send a GPA + major; Meridian returns the **Meridian-Adjusted GPA**.

---

### 3. The Radar Chart (Immediate Signal)

**The reality:** Recruiters spend an average of **6 seconds** on a resume.

**Why it sells:** Four years of a student’s life are condensed into **one geometric shape**. If the Grit and Build spikes are high, the recruiter knows instantly to keep reading.

**Expansion idea:** Make the Radar Chart **interactive**. A recruiter clicks the "Grit" spike and sees a **timeline of those 500+ clinical hours** instantly.

## Part 2 — The Most Advanced Parts (The "How")

*From advisor. The technical differentiators.*

### 1. Few-Shot Contextual Auditing

Using real resumes (e.g. 22+ gold-standard examples) to **"teach"** the model is far more advanced than a basic prompt. You are creating a **Local Brain**.

**The tech:** You aren’t asking the AI *"Is this person good?"* You are telling it: *"Here are 14 elite examples. Audit this new resume against these specific benchmarks."* This minimizes **AI hallucinations** and keeps scores **grounded in reality**.

**Next level:** Implement **Self-Correction Logic**. A second AI agent audits the first agent’s scores to ensure the 1.40x multiplier (and rest of Ground Truth logic) was applied correctly.

---

### 2. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) at Scale

Processing high token throughput isn’t just about speed—it’s about **synthesis**.

**The tech:** The model isn’t just "reading" text; it **retrieves specific behavioral patterns** across resumes and **cross-references** them with Ground Truth V6.5 logic.

**Next level:** Use **vector embeddings** to cluster candidates. The AI could say: *"This student has a Build profile that looks exactly like a Junior Dev at NVIDIA."*

---

### 3. Cross-Pillar Correlation

The most advanced part of the logic is where **Smart, Grit, and Build overlap**.

**The tech:** The system doesn’t treat these as three separate buckets. It looks for **interaction**. High Smart + High Grit + High Build = **The Unicorn Profile**.

**Next level:** Build a **"Predictive Success" Score**. Track where gold-standard students end up in 2 years. Use that data to train the auditor on which spike combinations actually lead to the best career outcomes.

## Part 4 — Complete Feature Set (Technical & Product)

*Everything the Meridian AI app features—current, in progress, and planned.*

### Scoring & Tracks

- **Ground Truth V6.5** — Canonical scoring logic (Smart, Grit, Build 0–100; composite final score).
- **Vantage Alpha tracks** — Pre-Health, Pre-Law, Tech, Science, Business, Communications, Education, Arts, Humanities; assigned from major + resume text (Pre-Health/Pre-Law from intent keywords). Humanities is the fallback for unknown majors.
- **Major multipliers (Rigor Calibration)** — 1.00–1.40 (and below-baseline for softer grading); research-backed per University of Tampa catalog; restores value of difficulty (the "1.40x logic").
- **Pre-Health BCPM** — Science GPA weighted when present; effective GPA formula for Smart base.
- **Minor bonus** — Research-backed points for catalog minors; multiple minors use highest.
- **Smart score** — GPA × 15 × major_multiplier + honors + research + minor; capped 0–100.
- **Grit score** — Quantifiable impact, leadership density, work entry count; international multiplier; capped 0–100.
- **Build score** — Track-specific (Tech: tech stack + projects; Pre-Health: clinical + research; Pre-Law: legal keywords + outcomes); capped 0–100.
- **Composite final score** — Track-weighted blend (e.g. 0.30 Smart + 0.45 Grit + 0.25 Build for Pre-Health/Humanities).

### Evidence & Transparency

- **Evidence-based audit** — Each dimension (Smart, Grit, Build) has evidence strings drawn from the resume; no generic phrases; cites specific roles, orgs, projects.
- **Evidence trail UI** — Click a dimension (Smart / Grit / Build) → see "Why you got your score" with the evidence that drove it.
- **Radar chart** — Single geometric view of Smart, Grit, Build; recruiter-ready signal in seconds.
- **Audit findings** — Narrative findings per dimension, grounded in parsed content (Meridian Truth Standard: no hallucination).
- **Red flags / anomaly detection** (planned) — e.g. High GPA + very low Build → "High-Risk / Low-Velocity" so students and recruiters see mismatches.

### Recommendations & Coaching

- **Personalized recommendations** — LLM-driven: generic advice, **line_edit** (exact current line → suggested line, same facts), **action** (concrete next steps + optional score target).
- **Training from every audit** — Each audited resume (with recommendations) is appended to few-shot data so the "Local Brain" improves over time.
- **Gap analysis & roadmap** (product vision) — Compare to top N in field; personalized 3-month plan to close gaps (e.g. missing hours, clubs, internships).

### Resume & Identity

- **Structured parsed resume** — Upload → parse → labeled sections ([EDUCATION], [EXPERIENCE], [LEADERSHIP], etc.); one canonical file per user; overwrite on re-upload.
- **Identity = email** — File and profile keyed by .edu email; filename e.g. `user@university.edu.txt` for sorting and clarity; no "Unknown" when email is available.
- **.edu-only auth** (planned) — Sign-in/sign-up with .edu only; verification code (no password); rate limit and expiry; resume upload only when logged in; upload overwrites that user’s file.
- **Section → dimension mapping** — Hybrid: default mapping in code (e.g. education/honors → Smart; experience/leadership → Grit; projects/clinical/tech → Build); LLM can refine per resume.

### Two-way dialogue, not just answers

- **Clarifying questions** — Before big advice: "Quick Q: Are you aiming for IB or markets? That changes what I'd suggest."
- **Option menus** — "I can (a) run a mock audit for this JD, (b) draft a cover letter opener, or (c) give you 3 interview prep bullets. Which?"
- **Confidence check** — "Does that feel doable, or do you want a smaller step first?"

---

### Rituals and "reasons to open the app"

- **"Daily tip" or "Today's focus"** — One line + one action (e.g. "Today: one number in every bullet.").
- **Weekly recap** — "This week you: ran 2 mock audits, updated 1 section. Next week: aim for one application with the new bullets."
- **Reminders that feel helpful** — "Application closes in 48 hours — want to do a final mock audit?"

---

### Voice and tone

- **Consistent persona** — Coach who's supportive but direct: "That bullet's vague. Here's a version that'll move the needle."
- **Encouragement + next step** — After a win: "That's a strong update. Next: get that on the Raymond James application."
- **No guilt trips** — If they've been away: "No worries. Whenever you're back, we can pick up from here."

---

### 1. Variable rewards (the "one more" feeling)

- **Notifications that feel valuable** — Job alerts, "New role at [Company you want]," "Your Grit moved into Top 10%," "Raymond James just posted." Same channel, different types of wins so it's not boring.
- **"What's new when I open the app?"** — A small feed: new matching jobs, score/percentile change, new recommendation, or "1 new tip for you." Creates a reason to open the app to see what's there.
- **Surprise wins** — Occasional "You unlocked X" or "New badge" (e.g. "First mock audit," "5 applications sent") so opening the app sometimes has a small "reward."
- **Why it sticks:** Brains are tuned to variable rewards. If something good might be there, people check more often.

---

### 2. Progress and commitment (I've already invested)

- **Visible progress** — "You're 2 steps from Elite," "3/5 roadmap items done," "80% ready for Goldman." Progress bars and checklists make people want to "finish."
- **Streaks** — "You've checked in 7 days in a row" or "7-day application streak." Optional, not punitive; breaking a streak feels like loss.
- **History** — "You've run 12 mock audits" / "You've updated your resume 4 times." Showing past effort makes them less likely to churn ("I've put a lot in here").
- **Why it sticks:** Sunk cost + clear "almost there" goals keep people coming back to complete things.

---

### 3. Identity ("I'm a Meridian user")

- **Badges / labels** — "Elite Pre-Health," "Top 10% Grit," "Verified Talent," "5 applications this month." Shareable or in-app; they become part of how the user sees themselves.
- **Language** — "Meridian users in the top 10%…" so they think "that's me." The AI says "your Grit," "your roadmap," so the product feels like their coach.
- **Community hint** — "You and 200+ UTampa students are using Meridian this week" (or similar). Light sense of "others like me use this."
- **Why it sticks:** When the product is part of their identity, quitting feels like losing part of who they are.

---

### 4. Loss aversion (don't miss out)

- **Deadlines** — "Application closes in 48 hours." "This role was just posted; similar roles fill in ~5 days." Urgency without fake scarcity.
- **"You'd be a fit"** — "This role matches your profile; 3 others applied today." FOMO that's real (job might close) not manufactured.
- **Score decay (optional, careful)** — E.g. "Resume not updated in 90 days; scores may be stale." Gentle nudge to re-engage, not punishment.
- **Why it sticks:** Fear of missing a real opportunity is a strong driver; keep it truthful.

---

### 5. Frictionless "one more" actions

- **One tap to do something** — "Run mock audit for this job?" "Add this to your roadmap?" "Mark applied?" So the next action is trivial.
- **AI that suggests the next step** — "Your next best move: add a number to this bullet." One tap or one short reply to "do the thing."
- **Quick wins** — "Today's 2-minute task: …" So even a short session feels productive.
- **Why it sticks:** Low friction + quick wins = "I'll just do this one thing" → repeated opens.

---

### 6. Daily hook (reason to open every day)

- **Morning or evening touchpoint** — One message: tip, nudge, or "1 thing for today." Same time or context (e.g. morning) so it becomes routine.
- **Daily micro-goal** — "Today: one bullet with a number" or "Today: run one mock audit." Check it off in the app.
- **Streak or consistency metric** — "5 days in a row" (optional, positive framing). Gives a reason to open the app "so I don't break my streak."
- **Why it sticks:** A single, predictable "moment" (e.g. morning Meridian check) becomes a habit.

---

### 7. Social proof and comparison (healthy)

- **Percentiles / leaderboard** — "You're in the top 10% for Grit." "You're ahead of 60% of Pre-Health candidates on Build." Optional, opt-in.
- **Norms** — "Most Meridian users who land roles apply within 48 hours of the posting." "Elite candidates usually have 3+ quantifiable bullets in Experience."
- **Stories / testimonials** — "Students like you who hit Elite got interviews at…" (anonymized). Makes the path feel real and achievable.
- **Why it sticks:** Comparison and "people like me succeed" increase motivation and relevance.

---

### 8. AI as the "person" they don't want to let down

- **Relationship, not just tool** — AI remembers goals, applications, and last conversation. "You said you'd add that bullet — did you?" (supportive, not naggy).
- **Celebration** — "You applied. That's the hardest part." "Your Build went up — here's what's working." Positive reinforcement.
- **Gentle accountability** — "Haven't seen you in a few days. Everything okay? When you're back we can…" So returning feels like reconnecting.
- **Why it sticks:** People maintain habits more when they feel a connection to someone (or something that feels like someone).
