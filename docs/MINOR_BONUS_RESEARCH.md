# Minor Bonus Points — Research Methodology & Justification

**Purpose:** The Smart score adds points when the resume states a **minor** (additional coursework; University of Tampa requires a minimum of 15 credit hours for a minor). This document records the research and process used to assign a bonus value for **every minor offered at the University of Tampa** (Catalog 2025–2026).

**Meridian Truth Standard:** Every bonus value must be backed by cited research. Minors in the same discipline as majors inherit the **relative rigor** established for that discipline in major-multiplier research. **Unlisted minor = +0** (no points without a research-backed value).

**Source of minor list:** University of Tampa Undergraduate Catalog 2025–2026, Areas of Study and college sections (Sykes College of Business, College of Arts and Letters, College of Natural and Health Sciences, College of Social Sciences, Mathematics and Education).  
- Catalog: https://ut.smartcatalogiq.com/en/current/catalog/undergraduate-catalog/the-utampa-undergraduate-academic-experience/minors  
- Areas of Study: https://ut.smartcatalogiq.com/en/current/catalog/areas-of-study/

---

## 1. Research policy

- **Basis:** Minor bonus tiers are derived from the **same evidence** used for major multipliers in `docs/MAJOR_MULTIPLIERS_RESEARCH.md`. A minor in Chemistry, for example, involves coursework in a discipline whose grading rigor and study-time burden are documented there; the minor bonus reflects that relative rigor.
- **Requirement:** Each minor’s tier (and thus point value) is justified by (1) mapping to the discipline’s major-multiplier tier and (2) at least one of the primary sources below (or the major doc §2 / §6).
- **Unlisted minor:** If a minor is not in this document and not in `MINOR_BONUS_PTS`, it receives **+0**. No invented values.

## 2. Primary research sources (shared with major multipliers)

These sources support **relative rigor by discipline** (STEM > quant/business > social science > humanities/communications/arts). Minors in a given discipline receive a bonus consistent with that discipline’s tier.

| # | Source | Type | Finding / use for minors |
|---|--------|------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Tomkin et al. (2022)** — “STEM courses are harder: evaluating inter-course grading disparities with a calibrated GPA model,” *International Journal of STEM Education*. https://stemeducationjournal.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40594-022-00343-1 | Peer-reviewed | STEM courses grade ~0.4 points more stringently than non-STEM. Minors in math, chemistry, physics, CS, data science, cybersecurity, biology receive highest bonus band (8–10). |
| 2 | **NSSE (National Survey of Student Engagement)** — Study hours per week by field. Physics ~19 hr/wk vs sociology ~13 hr/wk; engineering, biology, physical science/math/CS ~18+ hr/wk; social sciences ~16; business ~15.7. | Institutional / survey | Minors requiring quantitative or lab-heavy coursework (STEM) get higher bonus; communications/arts lower. |
| 3 | **Rojstaczer / Hermanowicz & Woodring** — “The Distribution of College Grades across Fields.” Humanities grade ~0.3 higher than natural sciences. | Peer-reviewed | Humanities, communications, arts minors placed in lower bonus band (3–5). |
| 4 | **UC Berkeley OPA** — GPA by major (2023–24). Applied Math 3.36, Chemistry 3.46, Physics 3.50; Sociology 3.47, English 3.65, Film 3.60, Dance 3.79. https://pages.github.berkeley.edu/OPA/our-berkeley/gpa-by-major.html | Institutional | Orders disciplines: math/chem/physics low (strict); arts/humanities higher. Minors mapped accordingly. |
| 5 | **BigEconomics / College Transitions / MyDegreeGuide** — “The Hardest and Easiest College Majors.” Chemistry, math, physics, engineering, CS, biology in “hard” tier; education, management, marketing, communications “easier.” https://bigeconomics.org/the-hardest-and-easiest-college-majors-full-list/ | Cited | Tier ordering for minors: STEM 8–10; quant/business 6–7; social science/languages 5; communications/arts 3–4. |
| 6 | **NCES** — Median GPAs by field; engineering/science/math lower than humanities/education. | Federal | Supports band assignment across minors. |
| 7 | **University of Tampa Catalog** — Minors require minimum 15 credit hours; at least 4 credits in residency. Same discipline as major often shares course rigor. | Institutional | Confirms all listed minors represent substantive additional work; tier comes from discipline rigor. |

## 3. Tier system (evidence-based bands)

| Tier | Bonus pts | Rationale (summary) |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| **1** | 9–10 | Hardest: mathematics, mathematics with computer science, chemistry, physics, computer science, data science, cybersecurity (STEM rigor per Tomkin, NSSE, Berkeley OPA, BigEconomics). |
| **2** | 7–8 | Hard STEM/quant/health: biology, marine biology, economics, accounting, forensic/environmental criminology; MIS, business analytics (quant/tech). |
| **3** | 5–6 | Mid: finance, criminology and criminal justice, law justice and advocacy, psychology, sociology, political science, international studies, history, geography, philosophy, languages (Spanish, French), applied linguistics, English, writing, professional and technical writing, area studies (Asian, Latin American and Caribbean, Black studies, women gender and sexuality studies), leadership studies, exercise science and sport studies, recreation, sustainability, professional education, military science. |
| **4** | 3–4 | Lower: communication, advertising, public relations, journalism, cinema studies, digital media, interactive media, speech studies, speech and theatre, art, design, visual arts, dance, music, theatre, business administration, management, marketing, professional selling. |

## 5. Implementation

- **Code:** `dilly_core/scoring.py` — `MINOR_BONUS_PTS` contains every UT minor above with the stated point value. `_normalize_minor_to_canonical()` maps resume phrases to these canonical names.
- **Unlisted:** Any minor not in this table and not in `MINOR_BONUS_PTS` receives **+0** (no bonus).
- **Multiple minors:** When more than one minor is detected, the **highest** bonus among them is used (single additive bonus).

---

## 6. Maintenance

- **Adding a new UT minor:** Add to this doc (§4) with tier, points, and one-line rationale citing §2; add to `MINOR_BONUS_PTS` and to `_normalize_minor_to_canonical()` in `scoring.py`.
- **Changing a value:** Update rationale in §4 and in code; ensure at least one source in §2 supports the tier.
- **New catalog:** When UT publishes a new catalog, reconcile the minor list with §4 and add any new minors with research-backed tier.
