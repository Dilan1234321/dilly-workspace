# Major Multipliers — Research Methodology & Justification

**Purpose:** The Smart score uses `GPA × 15 × major_multiplier`. Multipliers adjust for **grading rigor by discipline** so that the same GPA in a harder-grading major is not penalized relative to a softer-grading major. This document records the research and process used to set a multiplier for every University of Tampa major.

**Meridian Truth Standard:** Every multiplier must be backed by cited research. **No multiplier may be set or changed based on one or two articles alone.**

---

## Research Policy: Minimum 5 Sources Per Major (Scale to 10 When Meridian Gets Big)

- **Requirement:** Each major’s multiplier must be justified by a **minimum of 5 high-grade, industry-professional papers and/or sites**. **5 is the hard floor** (never below 5); we will scale to **10** when Meridian grows (e.g. enterprise, institutional contracts, or compliance needs). “High-grade” means: peer-reviewed journals, federal/institutional data (NCES, NSF, university OPA/IR), or widely cited policy/education research (e.g. Georgetown CEW, NCTQ, Education Next, AEI, Rojstaczer/gradeinflation.com).
- **Status:** Multipliers in the table below are in use. Majors marked **5-source complete** have ≥5 cited sources in §6. Majors not yet audited to 5 sources are **provisionally** evidence-based (initial research) and remain subject to the full audit; values may be refined as the audit is completed.
- **Ongoing:** When adding a new major or changing a multiplier, document ≥5 sources in §6 before finalizing.

---

## 1. Definition of the Multiplier

- **What it represents:** Relative **grading stringency** (and thus signal strength of GPA) by major. Higher multiplier = major in which grades are systematically lower for comparable ability (stricter grading, harder coursework, or both).
- **Why it matters:** Observed GPA is a biased measure across fields. Research shows STEM grades are ~0.3–0.4 points lower than non-STEM for academically similar students. Without adjustment, a 3.5 in Biochemistry would be undervalued vs. a 3.5 in Communication.
- **Baseline:** 1.00 = neutral (no adjustment). Multipliers range approximately 0.85–1.40.

## 2. Primary Research Sources

### 2.1 STEM vs. Non-STEM Grading Disparity (Calibrated GPA)

- **Source:** Tomkin et al. / Witteveen & Attewell; *International Journal of STEM Education* (2022): “STEM courses are harder: evaluating inter-course grading disparities with a calibrated GPA model.”
- **URL:** https://stemeducationjournal.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40594-022-00343-1
- **Finding:** Using a calibrated GPA (logistic model removing course-selection bias), **STEM courses grade ~0.4 grade points more stringently** than non-STEM. “When comparing otherwise academically similar students, STEM students have substantially lower grades and GPAs, and that this is the consequence of harder (more stringent) grading in STEM courses.”
- **Use:** Justifies elevating STEM/quant majors above 1.0 and grouping hardest-STEM (math, chemistry, physics, biochemistry) at the top tier.

### 2.2 Grade Distribution by Field (Humanities vs. Sciences)

- **Source:** Rojstaczer / Hermanowicz & Woodring; “The Distribution of College Grades across Fields in the Contemporary University” (Springer).
- **Finding:** Humanities grade ~0.3 points higher than natural sciences nationally; field “consensus” on standards correlates with rigor and grade allocation.
- **Use:** Justifies humanities/arts/communications at or below baseline (1.0 or 0.90–0.95).

### 2.3 Average GPA by Major (Institutional Data)

- **Source:** UC Berkeley OPA, “GPA by Major” (2023–24). Cited in College Transitions, “Average College GPA by Major – 2025.”
- **URL:** https://pages.github.berkeley.edu/OPA/our-berkeley/gpa-by-major.html (Berkeley); https://www.collegetransitions.com/blog/average-college-gpa-by-major/
- **Sample (lower GPA = stricter):** Applied Math 3.36, Civil Engineering 3.37, Chemistry 3.46, Physics 3.50, Sociology 3.47, Philosophy 3.49, Psychology 3.54, Political Science 3.55, History 3.64, English 3.65, Film 3.60, Music 3.69, Comparative Literature 3.70, Anthropology 3.73, Dance 3.79, Business Admin 3.62.
- **Use:** Orders majors within broad bands (math/chemistry/physics low; humanities/arts higher).

### 2.4 Hardest Majors (Difficulty & Attrition)

- **Sources:** MyDegreeGuide, College Transitions, BigEconomics (“The Hardest and Easiest College Majors”), HowStuffWorks (attrition).
- **Findings:** Chemistry often cited as hardest (lowest avg GPA ~2.78); Mathematics ~2.90; Physics, Engineering, Biology, Economics, Computer Science in “hard” tier. STEM attrition high (~50% leave STEM in 6 years). Education and “soft” business (e.g. management, marketing) ranked easier / higher GPA.
- **Use:** Tie-breaking and ordering within STEM (Biochemistry, Chemistry, Physics, Math at top) and placing Education / Management / Communication lower.

### 2.5 Education Major Grade Inflation

- **Source:** Education Next, “Want a 3.8 GPA? Major in Education”; AEI, “Grade Inflation for Education Majors and Low Standards for Teachers.”
- **Finding:** Education majors receive among the highest GPAs (e.g. 3.8 at Missouri) while scoring lower on entrance exams; “culture of low standards” documented.
- **Use:** Education majors (Secondary, Elementary, Music Education, Professional Education) set at 0.90–0.95.

### 2.6 Nursing & Health Majors

- **Source:** Nursing program admissions (GPA requirements, rigor of science prereqs); general consensus that nursing combines heavy science and clinical demands.
- **Finding:** BSN competitive (3.2–3.5+ typical); science prereqs graded stringently; nursing often listed among demanding majors.
- **Use:** Nursing, Allied Health, Biomedical Sciences, Public Health, Health Science, Human Performance in 1.10–1.15 range; Art Therapy (more applied/creative) slightly lower.

### 2.7 National Center for Education Statistics (NCES)

- **Source:** NCES report (student financial aid cohort); median GPAs by field.
- **Finding:** Engineering ~3.24, Nursing/health ~3.35, Education ~3.31; science/math lower than humanities/education.
- **Use:** Supports tier ordering across broad categories.

## 3. Tier System (Evidence-Based Bands)

| Tier | Multiplier range | Rationale (summary) |
|------|------------------|---------------------|
| **1** | 1.35 – 1.40 | Hardest grading / rigor: chemistry, math, physics, biochemistry (calibrated STEM gap + “hardest major” lists). |
| **2** | 1.25 – 1.30 | Hard STEM/quant: data science, computer science, actuarial, math with CS, cybersecurity (STEM rigor, lower GPAs). |
| **3** | 1.15 – 1.20 | Moderate STEM / health / quant: biology, nursing, marine/environmental/forensic science, finance, economics, BIT/MIS, financial enterprise systems. |
| **4** | 1.05 – 1.10 | Slightly above baseline: accounting, public health, health science, allied health, biomedical sciences, human performance, art therapy. |
| **5** | 1.00 | Baseline: pre-law (political science, history, philosophy, criminology, etc.), English, writing, sociology, Spanish, applied linguistics, psychology, neutral business (sport management). |
| **6** | 0.90 – 0.95 | Softer grading / higher GPAs typical: education, arts (design, film, music, theatre, etc.), marketing, international business, advertising/PR, journalism. |
| **7** | 0.85 – 0.90 | Highest grade inflation / lowest rigor signal: management, general communication (Communication, Communication and Media Studies, Communication and Speech Studies). |

## 7. Process Summary (Steps Taken)

1. **Defined the multiplier** — Grading rigor / signal strength of GPA; baseline 1.00.
