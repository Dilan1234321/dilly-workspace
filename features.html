Why “one score” is the spine

When every surface agrees on Smart, Grit, and Build, you stop translating between a resume grader, a job board, and a chatbot. Improvements in the editor show up in listings. Coach answers match your gap bars.

That integration is the product thesis: fewer tabs, fewer contradictions, faster iteration cycles while you still have semesters to work with.

  • Shared dimensions across audit, jobs, and coach
  • Tracker and calendar inherit context from saved roles
  • Leaderboard reflects the same signals you are optimizing

First thirty days

A realistic onboarding arc

  1. Week 1: Upload, score, ATS scan. Fix structural blockers before poetic edits.
  2. Week 2: Editor sprints on lowest bullets. Save five target roles; read gap labels honestly.
  3. Week 3: Promote two applications in tracker. Load deadlines into calendar. Run Ask Dilly on the top pick.
  4. Week 4: Coach mock block on behavioral gaps. Check leaderboard trend, not day noise.

Built for the ratio that broke career centers

Thousands of students per advisor means no one sees your file often enough to give line-level guidance. Dilly compresses repeated advisor moves into software: diagnose, prioritize, rehearse, and track.
